Ionic Bond

Ions

  • Ions are charged particles
  • Particles are charged when they gain/lose electrons to form a full outer shell
  • Noble gases are very difficult to become ions because they have a full outer shell and is very stable
Atom Gain/Loss Final Charge
Metals Loss Positive
Non-metals Gain Negative

Ionic Bond

  • When metal (positive) meets non-metal (negative), they will form ions that attracts each other because of their opposite charge
  • Positive ions and negative ions are attracted in a infinite space filling pattern (giant lattice)

Properties

  • Very strong bond, because is held together by the electostatic force of attraction
  • Since the bond is very strong, it has a high melting point (more energy is required to break the bonds)
