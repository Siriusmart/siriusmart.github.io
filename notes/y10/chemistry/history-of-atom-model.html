History of the Atom Model

1. Solid Atom (1803)

  • Suggests that all matter is made up of atoms
  • All atoms of an element are identical
  • Different elements contain different types of atoms

2. Plum-pudding model (1897)

  • Atoms contain electrons
  • Atoms are neutral overall

Notes

The model comes from the discovery of electron

3. Nuclear model (1909)

The experiment

Shoot alpha particles (positively charged) at a sheet of gold foil

Expectations

  • Particles should go straight through the pudding
  • Some particles might be nudged slightly

Results

  • Most particles went straight through
  • 1 out of 2000 deflected at angle smaller than 90
  • 1 out of 8000 bounced right back
  • Nucleus with strong concentrated charge
  • Electrons orbiting the nucleus
