Covalent Bond

  • Non-metal atoms shares electrons to form a full shell. > ### Note > Non-metals have an non-full outer shell with a few missing electrons
  • The number of electrons shared between 2 atoms must be the same
  • The electron is trapped in the area between the 2 atoms, and is attracted to both nuclei
  • Held together by the electostatic force of attraction, and is stronger than ionic bonds

Simple Covalent Structures

  • A simple covalent structure only have a few atoms (buckyball aka football have like 60 carbon atoms)
  • The molecules are bonded with strong covabent bonds, but they are not bonded to each other

CO₂ Dot and Cross Diagram

Properties

  • Weak intermolecular forces, they therefore have a low melting point (for example, water H₂O)

Giant Covalent Structures

  • Atoms joined by covalend bonds arranged in repeating patterns (a giant lattice)
  • Each atoms are covalently bonded to each other

Properties

  • High melting point because all atoms are bonded to each other

Note

  • In graphene, atoms within the same layer are bonded to each other but there are no bonds between the layers
