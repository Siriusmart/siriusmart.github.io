The Harber Process

Equation

N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) <=> 2NH₃(g)

-93kJ/mol

Conditions

Pressure:

  • 200 atmospheric pressure (20MPa)
  • Less moles to the right, so higher pressure = more yield
  • High pressure is dangerous

Temperature:

  • 450 degrees Celsius
  • Lower is better because it is an exothermic reaction
  • But higher temperature speeds up the reaction

Catalyst

  • Iron catalyst
  • Speeds up the reaction

Process

  1. Nitrogen and hydrogen goes under pressure
  2. Gas enters reaction vessel (450 degrees with beds of catalyst)
  3. Water cooled to condense ammonia to liquid
  4. Unreacted gas recycled

The yield is around 97%
