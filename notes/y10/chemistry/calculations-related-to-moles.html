Calculations related to moles

Concentration

Unit

mol/dm³ (moles per dm³)

Note

  • A dm is 10cm
  • A dm³ is 1000cm³ (aka a litre)

So bascially mol/dm³ is about how many of that stuff is there per litre.

Given the concentration and the total volume, you can calculate the number of moles. And from there you can calculate the mass of the stuff.

Just basic maths good morning.

Yield

Theoretical yield

Maximum possible amount of product with the given amount of reactants

Example

N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) -> 2NH₃(g)

If there is 1 mole of N₂ and 1 moles of H₂, then the maximum amount of NH₃ is 2 moles (Because of 2NH₃).

The first step to answer these kind of questions is to find the limiting factor, the number of moles of that limiting factor will be the maximum theoretical yield (in moles).
