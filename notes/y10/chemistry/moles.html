Moles

Mole is a unit of measurement for the number of atoms (or molecules).

For example, 1 mole of hydrogen is 1 gram and 1 mole of carbon is 12 grams, and there is a same number of hydrogen and carbon atoms in 1 mole.

The number of “thing” in 1 mole is called the Avogadro number (6.022 x 10²³).

Total mass

Atoms

To calculate the total mass of an atom, simple multiply the relative mass of the atom by the number of atoms (answer is in grams).

Mass(grams) = Aᵣ x Moles

Molecules

To calculate the total mass of a molecule, calculate the relative mass of each molecule first, then multiply by it by the number of moles.

Mass(grams) = Mᵣ x Moles
