Polar Case Study

Human activities

  • Indigious communities: Sami, Inuit
  • Scientific research
  • Oil drilling/Coal/Gas
  • Fishing (and whaling)
  • Tourism and trade

Note

Whaling has been banned from Arctic waters

Tourism

  • Cruise ships
  • Mountain climbing
  • Whale watching
  • Visit nomadic/indigenous peoples
  • See northern lights
  • Skiiling (Norway/Sweden/Finland)
  • Visit Santa (bruh)
  • Ice hotels

Impacts

Positive Negative
Tourists become ambassadors for Antarctica Invasive species brought by food and cargo
Disturb breeding birds
Erosion (e.g. leave paths)
Oil spills (in case of accident)

Examples

Union Glacier Camp

  • Different location every year: Doesn’t leave permanent damage
  • Short periods of time each year
  • Tourists don’t need to travel to Antarctica individually: Reduces carbon footprint

Historical conservation

  • Shackleton’s hut

Halley VI base

  • British Antartic base
  • Built on silts: Does not melt ice
  • Modular structure and have wheels: Can be transported easily

Antarctica treaty

  • Demilitarise zone
  • Nuclear-free zone
  • Freedom for scientific investigations
  • Observations and results from Antarctica are exchanged and made public
  • Logistic co-operation and communication to aid the safety of all
  • Protect the environment
  • Ban activities related to mineral resources
  • Visitors must comply with the treaty and protocol
  • Preserve historical sites
