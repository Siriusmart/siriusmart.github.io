Ethiopia Case Study

  • Ethiopia is a landlocked country in east Africa
  • Western highlands: Long rainy seasons good for crops
  • Central area: Unreliable rainfall
  • Eastern lowland: Flat plains, 2 wet and dry seasons, high temperature year round

Political history

1935

  • Italy colonized Ethiopia
  • Rebels and British troops gain back control shortly after

1941

  • Set back after WW2 due to conflicts and loss of life

1974

  • Communist Derg overthrew the government leading to unrest

1985

  • Ethiopia suffered drought and eventaully famine
  • Charities deliever food aid but the famine continued

1991

  • The Derg government was removed
  • New government allow free trade and provide farmers with cheaper access to machineries and fertilizers

2001

  • USA gave more support to Ethiopia and ecibiny have been gradually rising
  • International plans to end proverty provide training programmes allowing farmers to learn new skills
