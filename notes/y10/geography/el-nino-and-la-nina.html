El Nino and La Nina

Location: Pacific

Normal

  • Trade wind blows west pushing warm surface seawater to the west
  • Warm water pushed towards Australia causing rising air, low pressure and rainfall
  • Cold water at South America causing sinking cold air, high pressure and dry climate

Normal conditions

El Nino

  • Trade winds weakens or blows in reversed directions
  • Warm water is no longer pushed to the west and spreads all across the Pacific
  • Less rainfall at Australia and more rainfall at South America
  • More of the surface is warm water causing climate to warm up globally

Note

  • Water becomes 6 - 8 degrees warmer at the east (South America)

La Nina

  • Trade winds is stronger causing more warm water to be pushed to the west
  • Pushes warm water towards Austraila more
  • Cases dry climate in South America and more rainfall at Australia
