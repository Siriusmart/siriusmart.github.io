Impacts of Climate Change

Sea level rise

An average rise in sea level of 20 cm since 1900

Social impacts

  • 600 million people live in area that are less than 10m above sea level
  • People living in vulnerable areas may have to move
  • Increaed frequency of flooding
  • Environmental refugees

Economic impacts

  • Agricultural land polluted by seawater
  • Harours and ports may be affected
  • Transport systems may be damaged
  • Requires coastal defense to protect property
  • Impacts coastal tourism

Environmental impacts

  • Polluted fresh water sources (salinisation)
  • Damage to coastal ecosystem
  • Loss of coastal wetland
  • Harbours may be blocked by sediment due to increased rate of erosion

Case study: Tuvalu

Location: Small islands in the South Pacific

Impacts from rising sea level

  • Highest point is only 4.5m above sea level
  • Salinisation
  • Coastal erosion
  • Floods

Extreme weather

  • There is more energy in the atmosphere
  • Storms are more powerful and frequent
  • Climate patterns may shift

Case study: Brazillian drought

Location: Brazil

Time: 2014

  • Record breaking dry season
  • Cities face water shortages
  • Affects industial production and farming
  • Damaging natural ecosystems and kills fish
