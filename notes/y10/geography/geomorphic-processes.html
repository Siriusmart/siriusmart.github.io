Geomorphic Processes

Weathering

Breaking down of material in situ (no material is transported away)

Physical

Freeze-thaw cycle

Requirements: Temperature goes below and above freezing point

  1. Water seeps into cracks and fractures in the rock
  2. Water expands 9% when freezing, expanding the gap
  3. Repeat and rock breaks into pieces

Rock fragments at the bottom of the valley are called scree

Exfoliation (Onion-skin)

Requirements: High temperature difference (usually in high altitudes)

Day

  1. Outer layer got heated up by the sun, while the rock is still cool
  2. The outer layer expands more then the inner parts

Night

  1. Outer layer cools, while the rock is still warm
  2. The outer layer contracts more than the inner parts

Biological

  • Roots growth crack rocks
  • Lichen and moss grow on rocks, and eat away the surface

Erosion

Wearing away/removing of material

Hydraulic action

Air forced into cracks, creating a surge of high pressure

Abrasion

Wave throws sediment against the base of a cliff

Attrision

Rocks crashing into each other creating finer fragments

Solution

Slowly dissolved by acidic seawater

Transportation

Mass movement is the process of moving a large amount of material from one place to another

Rockfall

Description: Rain washes away loose materials

Landslide

Requirements: Layers of rock tilting towards the sea

  1. Crack in the top layer
  2. Water seeps down and the second layer is impermeable
  3. Water acts as lubricant, and the top layer slides into the sea

Strata: Horizontal layers

Rotational slumping

Requirements: Layers of softer permeable rock on top of harder impermeable rock

  1. Heavy rainfall saturates the soil, which add weight to the cliff
  2. Water seeps down the soil and rocks
  3. Water lubricates the bounding between layers
  4. Abrasion erodes the base of the cliff, weakening it from below
  5. Weee!
