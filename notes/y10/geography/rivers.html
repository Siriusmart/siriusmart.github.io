Rivers

Geomorphic processes

  • The greater the mass of water the more energy it has
  • The higher the river is above sealevel the more energy it has
  • The steeper the river channel is the faster the river flows

Energy level

Energy level What happens
Too high Erosion
In between Nothing
Too low Deposition

Erosion

Abrasion

  • Effective in short turbulent periods
  • Happens when the river is at bankfull or in flood

Hydraulic action

  • Movement of loose material due to force of water and air into cracks
  • Effective when the flow is fast
  • Strong in rapids and waterfalls

Corrosion/Solution

  • Effects rocks that contains carbonate

Deposition

Material is deposited when there is a decrease in velocity or volume

  • Velocity decrease when river enters a lake or sea
  • Decrease in gradient (downstream) causes velocity to decrease
  • Droughts cause volume of water to decrease
  • Low velocity when spreading out in a flood
