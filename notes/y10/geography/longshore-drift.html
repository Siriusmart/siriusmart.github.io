Longshore Drift

Transportation of materials

Traction

Large rock rolls on the seabed

Saltation

Pebbles bounces on the seabed by the waves

Suspension

Sediment held in water

Solution

Dissolved sediment in water

Water color Rocks
White Chalk, limestone
Brown Clay, mudstone, sand

Process of longshore drift

  1. Prevaling wind at 45 degrees angle to the direction of the coast
  2. Material is pushed up at the wind direction
  3. Backwash perpendicular to the coast
  4. Material is moved along the coast

Beaches

Beaches are formed when the rate of deposition is more than erosion

Shingle beaches are steeper because backwash can percolate through the shingles

Spits

When longshore drift meets a sudden turn in the coastline, the materials are deposited into the sea (not on the coastline)

Requires calm water

Bar Tombolo
Spit continues to the other side sealing off a bay Spit hits an island connecting it to land

Note

Area behind a bar is called a lagoon (will turn into a salt marsh due to evaporation of trapped water)
