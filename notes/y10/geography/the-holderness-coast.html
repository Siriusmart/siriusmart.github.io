The Holderness Coast

  • Fastest eroding coastline in the UK
  • East UK
  • Erodes 0.5 - 2m per year
  • Waves coming from northeast
  • Wave fetch around 2000km
  • Longshore drift direction: North to south

1. Flamborough Head

  • Material: chalk (resistant)
  • Features: Arch, stack, stump, wave cut platform
  • Hard to erode because of resistant rocks (very slow erosion)

2. Hornsea

  • Tourist town
  • Recieved protection after cost-benefit analysis

Coastal protections

Protection Description
Concrete seawall Wall of concrete…
Groynes Wooden boards sticking into the sea
Sand dunes Bunch of sand (needs replenishing very often)
Rip rap Large rocks before the seawalls to absorb wave energy

Terminal groyne syndrome

  • A large erosion curve-in after the last groyne
  • Caused by the missing material coming from the protected area
  • Increased rate of erosion

3. Mappleton

  • Recieved protection because there is a major road nearby

Protections

4. Aldbrough

  • Small village
  • No reason to protect
  • Because there is no coastal protection, and made worse by the protection to the north
  • Erosion rate: 1.5m per year

Spurn head

  • Where is materials are deposited
  • No reason to protect because it doesn’t need protection (it’s growing from the sediments)
  • With the coastal protections to the north the spurn head starts to erode away
