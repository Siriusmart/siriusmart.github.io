Historical Climate

Quaternary geological period

  • The past 2.6 million years
  • Temperature fluctuated wildly
  • Glacial periods with warmer inter-glacial periods in between
  • Gradual overall cooling

Evidence for climate change

  1. Geological fossil evidence

  • Plants and animals species only live in a certain climate
  • Fossils can be used to find the climate of that location at a certain time

  1. Ice cores

  • Ice cores from Greenland and Antarctica ice sheet shows climates of past 400,000 years
  • Layers of snow is compressed into ice (deeper is older)
  • Temperature can be calculated by analysing trapped water molecules

  1. Ocean sediments

  • Ocean sediments build up over years
  • Some sediment up to 5 million years old
  • Past temperatures can be calculated by studying trapped oxygen isotopes

  1. Historical records

  • Most untrustworthy
  • Ancient cave paintings of animals
  • Records of extreme weather show the frequency

Events

The Little Ice Age (1300 - 1870)

  • Price of grain increased
  • Sea froze
  • Limited food supplies

Recent evidence for climate change

1. Global temperature data

  • Average global temperature increased by 0.6 degrees Celsius since 1950 and 0.85 degrees Celsius since 1980
  • Very accuarate and reliable

2. Shrinking ice sheets and glaciers

  • Retreating ice sheets and glaciers are the results of global warming

3. Sea level change

  • Sea level risen by 10 - 20 cm in the past 100 years
  • Caused by melting glaciers and ice caps
  • Thermal expansion of the ocean
