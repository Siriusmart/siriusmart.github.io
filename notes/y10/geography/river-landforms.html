River Landforms

Upper course

  • V-shaped valley and interblocking spurs
  • Waterfalls and gorges
  • Riffles and pools

Valleys

  1. Channel was eroded downwards due to gavitational potential energy
  2. Creates a deep river valley
  3. Freeze-thaw weathering weakens sides of the channel
  4. Sides of the channel collapse, leaving a v-shaped valley
  5. River flow in path of least resistant and move from side to side avoiding harder rocks, forming the interblocking spurs

Waterfalls

  1. Waterfall occurs where more resistant rock sits on top of less resistant rocks
  2. Softer rock erodes through hydraulic action and abrasion
  3. This undercuts at the base of the waterfall resulting in an overhand and a plunge pool
  4. The top harder rock collapses because there is not enough support
  5. Fallen rocks broken into pieces by attrition and solution
  6. As the waterfall retreats, it cuts a deep, steep valley

Riffles and pools

  • More resistant parts of the river channel has an uneven surface and erodes very slowly
  • Less resistant parts erodes away forming pools

Middle course

  • Meanders and ox bow lakes
  1. The most erosion occurs at the thalweg

Thalweg

  • The thalweg is the fast flow in a river channel
  • It oscillates from side to side
  1. Erosion undercuts the river cliffs
  2. River cliff collapses due to mass movement
  3. The oscillation of the thalweg causes the erosion to be uneven, forming curves
  4. The curvature increases forming a meander neck where the river channel is close to the channel other side in the curve
  5. In a flood water goes in straight paths cutting the meander neck
  6. The straight flow increases flow speed causing more erosion and deepens the new channel
  7. Eventually, the build up of sediment cuts off the meander bend entriely forming an ox bow lake

Lower course

  • Wide, shallow river valley
  • Floodplanes and levees
  • Braided channels

Floodpanes

  1. Natural river process causes overspill banks in high discharge
  2. Water flows across the flood plain until it reaches higher land
  3. Deposits fertilize soil

Levees

  1. In a flood largest, heaviest objects are deposited nearest to the river banks (because they can travel the least distance)
  2. These objects are deposited in levees

Braided channels

Location: Close to the mouth or delta

  • Small islands or eyots of deposited materials within the channel
  • Network of small interwinding channels covering and diverge areound the eyots

Eyot

Small island in the channel
