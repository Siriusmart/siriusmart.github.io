Units of Electricity

Electrical Charge: Coulombs (c)

  • Measure the amount of electrical charge (amount of electrons)
  • A coulomb is approx. 6 x 10¹⁸ electrons worth of charge

Current: Amps (A)

  • The rate of electrical charge flow (amount of flow per second)
  • I = Q/t (Current = Charge/Time)

Voltage: Volts (V)

  • The push of the current (how much energy each electron has)
  • V = W/Q (Voltage = Work done (J)/Charge)

Resistance: Ohms (Ω)

  • V = IR (Voltage = Current * Resistance)
