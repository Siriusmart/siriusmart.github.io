Decay

Note

The particles created in the decay process shoots out at high speed.

Types of radioactive decay

Alpha decay

  • -2 atomic number (protons)
  • -4 mass number (neutrons plus protons)

Beta decay

  • Does not change mass number (because the mass of an electron is negligible)
  • +1 atomic number (because atomic number is the amount of positive charge an atom has)

Gamma radiation

  • Does not change mass number or atomic number (because nothing was shoot out)
  • Changes its structure to get rid of the excess energy
  • Gamma ray is a high energy electromagnetic wave

Units

  • Dose: Sieverts
  • Rate: Becquerels

Half life

  • The time takes for 50% of atoms to decay
  • The decay rate of an atom is completely random and not affected by the environment
  • With a large amount of atoms, the decay rate can be predicted
