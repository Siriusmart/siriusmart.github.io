Other Types of Businesses

Franchises

  • An agreement where the franchisor sells rights to the franchisee allowing them to sell products/use the company name
  • Gives the rights to sell its products in return for a fixed franchise fee

Help provided by franchisor

  • Training of staff
  • Equipments/supplies
  • Help finding customers (because of brand)
  • Back up services (e.g. advice, loans)
  • Advertising
  • The brand name
  • Exclusive sales area (no other franchisees can set up a shop around you)

Cost

  • Franchise fee - Fixed
  • Royalty payments - % of profit

Not For Profit Organisation

  • Include communities, voluntary organisations and charities
  • Set up to achieve objectives other than profit
  • At least 50% of the organisation’s surplus income must be reinvested back into the enterprise or activities

Social enterprises

  • Business driven by a social or environmental purpose
  • Profits they made reinvested towards achieving that purpose

Benefits

  • Have a social or environmental aim at core
  • Put surplus profits back into the business or community
  • Provide employment opportunities (especially for people with special needs or criminal record)
  • Offer a more flexible approach to work
