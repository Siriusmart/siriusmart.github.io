Limited Companies

  • Limited liability
  • Capical is divided into shares (each shareholder holds a number of shares)
  • Directors (elected by shareholders) run the company

Private limited companies (Ltd)

  • Shares can only be transferred privately
  • All shareholders must agree on the transfer

Limited liability

  • Shareholders not responsible for the company’s debt
  • Shareholders may only lose the money they have invested in the company
  • The liability of shareholders is limited to the value of their investment into the company’s shares
Advantages Disadvantages
Limited liability Greater admin costs
Easier to raise finance/debt (selling shares) Public disclosure of company info (annual reports)
Stable structure (business continues to exist even when shareholders change)
Provides more privacy than a PLC

Public limited companies (PLC)

  • Can raise money through listing its shares on the stock exchange
  • Shares can be bought by the public
  • If someone buys up more than 50% shares they owns the business (takeover)
Advantages Disadvantages
Limited liability Shareholders have to agree on how profit is distributed
Raise large sums of money through selling shares to public Greater administrative costs
Continues to exist when shareholders change Finance limited to stock market valuation
Public can see company info and accounts
Risk of being taken over
Take large sums of money to set up
