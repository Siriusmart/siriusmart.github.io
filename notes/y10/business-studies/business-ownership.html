Business Ownership

Sole traders

  • Business that is owned and controlled by one person
  • Most businesses in the UK are small businesses, most of them normally operate as a sole trader (e.g. gardeners, plumbers, etc)
  • A sole trader can employ people, but the employers don’t share the ownership

Ownership

  • There is no difference in law between the the business and the person owning it
  • Legally the owner is the business
  • If the owner dies there is no business

Setup

  • Very easy to setup
  • Need to keep proper business accounts
Advantages Disadvantages
Owner gets to keep all the income Unlimited liability
Easy to setup and run Less capital
No need ot publish accounting results Heavy workload
No disagreements when making decisions Difficult to raise finance
Flexible Limited economies of scale
Lack of continuity

Partnerships

  • Has 2 to unlimited number of partners
  • All partners have unlimited liability

Deed of partnership

  • Legal contract which governs how a partnership will be owned an organised
  • Capical, liability, votes, profits, etc

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

  • Gives the benefits of limited liability
  • The partnership is responsible for debts rather than the individual partners

Limited Liability

  • Separation between the business and the shareholders
  • The most amount that they can loose is limited to how many shares they have in the business
Advantages Disadvantages
More capital Disagreements may happen
Easy to setup Profits have to be split
Affairs of partnerships are private Unlimited liability except LLPs
Losses will be shared
